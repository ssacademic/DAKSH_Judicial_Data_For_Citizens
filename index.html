The wait

How long did it actually take?

Each circle below represents roughly 200 cases. Colour shows how long those cases took to resolve.

📌 The modal experience is 1–2 years — more cases fall in this band than any other. The typical person waits about 577 days. 1 in 5 waited longer than 3 years.
⚠️ These are closed cases only. Cases still pending as of January 2021 are not in this dataset. The full picture of how long cases take cannot be determined from this data alone.

The spread

Not all cases experience the same system

Courts were grouped by their median resolution time. The bar below shows how cases are distributed across these groups — width is proportional to the number of cases.

📌 56% of all cases went through courts whose median resolution time falls in the middle band (roughly 1–2 years). A meaningful minority — 22% — went through the two slower groups. The causes of this variation — case complexity, workload distribution, other factors — are not visible in this dataset.

The record

What happened when cases closed?

Each square below represents 1% of all cases. Dark squares have a recorded outcome. Grey squares do not.

74% of cases have no recorded outcome in NJDG — the case was closed, but how it was closed was never entered. Only 26% have any outcome detail.

⚠️ This is a data completeness finding, not a legal one. The outcome may have been recorded in physical records — it simply was not entered into NJDG.

Key limitations
